# Case study: UK petrol forecourts via Jersey

Second piece of the three-case-study GoldenMatch package
([overview](00_package_overview.md)). The same open-source
pipeline that surfaced the 128 Ebury Street finding is applied
to a specific UK industry sector. The result: **the UK's two
largest petrol-forecourt operators (Motor Fuel Group, Rontec)
and the LSE-listed Tritax Big Box REIT all hold UK property
through Jersey-incorporated companies**, several of which
appear in the 2017 ICIJ Paradise Papers leak (Appleby).

**Target length:** 8–10 minutes.
**Tone:** Methodology + sector-specific. No accusation. Every
named company is a regulated UK entity or LSE-listed issuer;
the story is the structure, not misconduct.
**Audience:** Sector journalists (Real Deal, Property Week,
Forecourt Trader), competition regulators (CMA), HMT review
teams.

## Act 1 — The hook (0:00–1:00)

### Shot 1.1 — Cold open (0:00–0:15)

**Visual:** Drone footage of a UK motorway-service-station
forecourt (B&Q tier brand, generic). Slow zoom out to show
the bigger UK industrial landscape.

**Narration (VO):**
> Every time you fill up at a Morrisons-branded petrol station
> in the UK — and there are roughly 350 of them — the
> property under your feet is owned by a company called
> "Mercury PFS Newco 2 Limited", which is incorporated in
> Jersey, not the UK. Six hundred and twenty-nine UK
> forecourts and car parks are held by that single company.
> This isn't unusual. The UK's largest petrol-forecourt
> operator (Motor Fuel Group), its largest competitor (Rontec),
> and the largest logistics REIT on the London Stock Exchange
> (Tritax Big Box) all own their UK property the same way.

### Shot 1.2 — Title card (0:15–0:22)

```
THE FORECOURT–JERSEY ROUTE
How three UK property roll-ups hold their assets
```

### Shot 1.3 — The setup (0:22–1:00)

**Visual:** Side-by-side branding panels showing
- MFG logo + petrol-forecourt photo
- Rontec logo + petrol-forecourt photo
- Tritax Big Box REIT logo + warehouse photo

**Narration:**
> All three are regulated UK operating businesses. Motor Fuel
> Group is owned by US private equity firm Clayton, Dubilier &
> Rice; it bought Morrisons' forecourts for two-and-a-half
> billion pounds in 2022. Rontec is owned by Gerald Ronson's
> Heron International. Tritax Big Box REIT is a constituent of
> the FTSE 250 with a market capitalisation around three-and-

> these companies has done anything illegal. The story is
> *where* their UK property titles are registered, *who*
> administers the offshore vehicles that hold them, and what
> the public-data pipeline reveals about the scale.

## Act 3 — The findings (2:30–7:00)

### Shot 3.1 — Motor Fuel Group via Mercury PFS Newco 2 (2:30–3:30)

**Visual:** Animated graph:
```
MOTOR FUEL GROUP (UK operating business, MFG)
   ▼ owned by
CLAYTON, DUBILIER & RICE (US private equity)
   ▼ via
MERCURY PFS NEWCO 2 LIMITED (Jersey incorporation)
   ▼ administered through
10 BRICKET ROAD, ST ALBANS (single proprietor for all 629 titles)
   ▼ holds
629 UK PETROL FORECOURTS + CAR PARKS
   (per HMLR OCOD May 2026)
```

**Narration:**
> Motor Fuel Group's UK forecourt portfolio is held through a
> single Jersey-incorporated company, Mercury PFS Newco 2
> Limited, which administers six hundred and twenty-nine UK
> property titles from a single St Albans correspondence
> address — 10 Bricket Road. Three hundred and fifty-nine
> million pounds in recorded transaction values. None of these
> structures are illegal; the use of Jersey incorporation for
> UK property holdings is a standard PE-fund practice. But the
> *scale* — six hundred and twenty-nine sites administered as
> a single Jersey-incorporated holding — is the story.

### Shot 3.2 — Rontec via numbered SPVs in Paradise Papers (3:30–4:30)

**Visual:** Animated panel showing
```
RONTEC GROUP (Heron International)
   ▼ via at least
RONTEC SERVICE STATION 1A LIMITED + 10 numbered series + ...
   ▼ administered through
26 NEW STREET, JERSEY  (Appleby)
   ▼ documented in
2017 ICIJ PARADISE PAPERS LEAK
```

**Narration:**
> Rontec, the UK's second-largest forecourt operator, follows
> a similar Jersey-incorporated pattern. Their numbered
> "RONTEC SERVICE STATION" SPVs appear in the 2017 Paradise
> Papers leak — meaning their Appleby-administered Jersey
> incorporation documents were among those Mossack Fonseca's
> 2016 leak and Appleby's 2017 leak respectively published.
> The structure is legal. The structure also continues to
> hold UK forecourt property in 2026.

### Shot 3.3 — The "CHIS" numbered series (4:30–5:30)

**Visual:** Counter showing the CHIS series: CHIS 1, CHIS 1A,
CHIS 2, CHIS 2A, CHIS 3, CHIS 3A, ... through CHIS 12A.
Twenty-four numbered companies plus suffix variants.

**Narration:**
> The pipeline surfaces a numbered series of Jersey-incorporated
> companies called CHIS — twenty-four entities, each
> administered at the same 26 New Street, Jersey address as
> Rontec. CHIS 1, 1A, 2, 2A, 3, 3A, and so on through CHIS 12A.
> All in the Paradise Papers. All currently holding UK property
> per HMLR OCOD. CHIS as a series name is undocumented; it may
> be an acronym for "Convenience-Highway-Income-Securities" or
> similar; the pipeline doesn't tell us. What the pipeline does
> tell us is that *each* CHIS company is administered by the
> same Jersey law firm and registered to the same Jersey
> street address. That's how serial corporate structures are
> built — number them, register them sequentially, share the
> administrative apparatus.

### Shot 3.4 — Tritax Big Box REIT — the LSE-listed branch (5:30–6:30)

**Visual:** Tritax Big Box REIT plc logo + LSE ticker
"BBOX.L" + FTSE 250 constituent badge.

**Narration:**
> Tritax Big Box REIT plc is a constituent of the FTSE 250.
> Its UK industrial-logistics property — the cavernous
> warehouses that handle UK e-commerce fulfilment — is held
> in part through Jersey-incorporated SPVs called TRITAX
> ACQUISITION 4, 5, and 10. All three appear in the same 2017
> Paradise Papers leak. All three are administered at the
> same 26 New Street, Jersey address. Tritax Big Box REIT's
> board, its regulator the Financial Conduct Authority, and
> the LSE all already know the Jersey holding structure
> exists; it's in the published statutory accounts. What this
> video adds is the cross-link: these Tritax acquisitions are
> in the *same Paradise Papers leak* as the Rontec forecourts.

> Including assets owned by an LSE-listed REIT and the UK's

## Act 4 — The honest framing (7:00–8:30)

### Shot 4.1 — What this video does and does NOT establish (7:00–7:30)

**Visual:** Plain text card.
```
What this video does NOT establish:
 - That MFG, Rontec, or Tritax Big Box REIT have done
   anything illegal.
 - That Appleby or 26 New Street Jersey have failed any
   regulatory duty.
 - That Jersey-incorporated property holdings for UK
   operating businesses are improper.

What this video DOES establish:
 - The scale of UK property administered via single Jersey
   addresses, surfaced from public data in 5 minutes of
   compute.
 - The cross-link between LSE-listed and PE-backed UK
   operating businesses and 2017 Paradise Papers entities.
 - That the pipeline reproducibly identifies these patterns
   without requiring any private information.
```

### Shot 4.2 — The regulatory question (7:30–8:30)

**Visual:** Quote-card from the UK Treasury's 2022
beneficial-ownership white paper or similar published policy
text on transparency.

**Narration:**
> The UK already requires foreign-incorporated property
> owners to declare beneficial ownership via the 2022
> Economic Crime Act Register of Overseas Entities. Most of
> the named companies in this video are compliant — Mercury
> PFS Newco 2, Rontec Service Station 1A, the CHIS series,
> Tritax Acquisition vehicles. Their beneficial-owner
> declarations are public. The regulatory question this
> video raises is not "is the disclosure happening" — it is
> "what does the disclosure show, in aggregate, once
> joined." Per HMLR OCOD May 2026, the aggregate shows
> twenty-six New Street, Jersey alone hosts ~£3 billion of
> UK property administered through one law firm's vehicles.
> The shared series methodology page documents the bundle
> for verification.
