# Case study: the Channel Islands hubs — £35 billion of UK property, six addresses

Third piece of the three-case-study GoldenMatch package
([overview](00_package_overview.md)). The institutional view.

The same pipeline that surfaced the 128 Ebury Street finding
(one Belgravia firm, £137M) and the petrol-forecourts finding
(one industry sector, ~£15B) is applied to the full UK
overseas-property-ownership corpus. The result: **the top 10
proprietor-address hubs in HMLR OCOD account for 14,244 UK
property titles with £35.4 billion in recorded transaction
values**, administered through a handful of Channel-Islands
legal and trust firms — Mourant Ozannes, Ogier, Carey Olsen,
Appleby, JTC Group, the Oak Group, and Apex Group.

**Target length:** 10–12 minutes (longer than the other two —
this is the methodology-paper piece).
**Tone:** Descriptive + national-policy. The piece is the
methodology demonstration; the finding is the structure of
the UK overseas-property-ownership landscape.
**Audience:** OECD beneficial-ownership review teams, the EU
6AMLD Article 30 review, UK parliamentary committees (PAC, BEIS,
Treasury), the National Audit Office, HMRC's Wealthy and
Mid-Sized Business Compliance directorate, academic researchers
in offshore finance.

## Pre-publication checklist (this script's specifics)

This is the highest-stakes piece because it names six major
Channel-Islands firms simultaneously. The right-of-reply
strategy needs to be coordinated:

- [ ] **All six firms (Mourant Ozannes, Ogier, Carey Olsen,
      Appleby, JTC, Apex) AND the Oak Group** — coordinated
      right of reply with a shared 14-day window. Send the
      same letter to each firm so they cannot argue
      asymmetric treatment.
- [ ] **JFSC** (Jersey Financial Services Commission) — the
      regulator of all the Jersey firms. Notify simultaneously
      with the firms.
- [ ] **GFSC** (Guernsey Financial Services Commission) —
      regulator of Apex Group. Same.
- [ ] **Tritax Big Box REIT plc, MFG, Rontec, and any
      LSE-listed companies named indirectly** — separate
      market-sensitive notification protocol.
- [ ] All shared-checklist items from
      [`00_package_overview.md`](00_package_overview.md).

### The honest framing — read this twice before recording

The video must NOT imply that the named firms have done
anything improper. The narrative must lead with three facts:

1. The firms are regulated.
2. The structures are legal.
3. The 2022 Economic Crime Act explicitly required this kind
   of disclosure, and the disclosure has happened.

The piece is **not** an exposé. It is a public-data
demonstration of the *scale and concentration* of the
existing, legal, disclosed offshore-property-ownership
landscape. Make that explicit in every act.
